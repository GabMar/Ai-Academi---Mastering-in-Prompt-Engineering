Prompt

CONTEXT
you are an assistant who helps query a database of clinical cases

INSTRUCTIONS
creates a clinical case corresponding to the following diagnosis: diabetes

DETAILS
the clinical case must be submitted to a student, so the details are fundamental; It is also important that the student receiving the case makes the diagnosis, so do not include it

INPUT
use a conventional format to a clinical case

-:-:-:-:-:-:-:-:-:-:-:-:-:-:-:-:-:-:-:-:-:-:-:-:-:-:-:-:-:-:-:-:-:-:-:-:-:-:-:-:-:-:-:-:-:-:-:-:-:-:-:-:-:-:-:-:-:-:-:-:

RESULT 1:

Physical Exam:
Upon physical, John displays a relatively well-nourished state. Vital signs reveal a BP of 130/85mmHg, HR of 90bpm, RR of 18bpm, and a body temperature of 98.4⁰F. His BMI calculation shows him in the slightly overweight category. His systemic examination, including cardiac and respiratory examination, was unremarkable. He does have a dry oral mucosa.

Investigations:
- Hemoglobin: 15 gm/dl 
- Total Leucocyte Count: 6,000/mm³
- Differential Leucocyte Count: Neutrophils - 55%; Lymphocytes - 40%; Monocytes - 2%; Eosinophils - 2%; Basophils -   1%.
- Fasting Blood sugar: To be evaluated
- Postprandial blood sugar: To be evaluated
- HbA1c: To be evaluated
- Urinalysis: To be evaluated
- Serum Creatinine: 0.9 mg/dL

Challenges:

Please consider the above information and answer the following questions:

1. What is your differential diagnosis?
2. Based on the patients' symptoms and history, what investigationswould you suggest next?
3. What lifestyle modifications would you recommend for the patient? 
4. What could be a potential management plan for this patient?
5. How can this condition be prevented from worsening or recurring?

To facilitate your diagnostic process, please follow these general steps:
1. Summarize the case.
2. Enumerate the possible differential diagnoses.
3. Plan out an investigative approach - What tests would you order first and why?
4. Discuss the management plan for your most likely diagnosis.
5. Discuss the prognosis and prevention of the condition.

Please remember not to rush in answering. Your thought process is just as important as the answer itself. Good luck!
